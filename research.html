Projects (Research)

Why Paper Straws Suck: Bamboo Straw Supremacy

Plastic straws, while durable and easy to manufacture, is negatively impacting the environment when it is deposed of imporperly. This research paper aims to find whether plastic straw alternatives, namely bamboo, are viable along with their benefits and downsides.

See More

The research was conducted as a part of the MITES Semester (Minority Introduction to Engineering and Science), formerly MOSTEC (MIT Online Science Technology and Engineering Community), for the material science project course. Paper straws and bamboo straws were tested for their impact resistance, water absorbity, and many other properties that might impact the use of the product. If you want to learn more about the project, the PDF of the research paper is linked down below.

The Impact of Emotion Expressed in Words and Tone-of-Voice on Grains of Rice

Tested the impact of words spoken in different emotions expressed in the tone-of-voice on grains of rice. My sister, who worked on the project with me, and I hypothesized that the only the emotions impacts the grains of rice.

See More

Inspired by other research done on the impact of emotions expressed in words and the tone-of-voice on other non-human subjects, such as water and other plant products, my sister and I worked on this project together to test if the results are reproduceable in grains of rice as well.
